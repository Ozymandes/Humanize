Methodology
The study will adopt a qualitative multiple-case study design. Data will be collected through in-depth, semi-structured interviews with leadership teams from six to eight Egyptian digital tourism startups, including both active or scaling ventures and recently failed ones. To capture multiple perspectives within each firm, interviews will be conducted with both business leaders (such as CEOs) and technical leaders (such as CTOs or Heads of AI).
Justification
A qualitative case study approach is the most appropriate methodology for this research context. As emphasized by D’Andrea et al. (2023), ecosystem-driven success and failure are deeply embedded in local realities that cannot be adequately captured through standardized surveys. While a quantitative approach might indicate that startups lack venture capital, it would not reveal how founders compensate for this lack through informal financing, personal networks, or unconventional hiring practices.
By examining real teams building complex AI systems under constrained conditions, this study aims to uncover the practical “how” and “why” behind startup survival in emerging markets. Interviewing both business and technical leaders further ensures a holistic understanding of how modern high-tech ventures adapt when their ecosystems fail to provide the foundational support typically assumed in the literature.
